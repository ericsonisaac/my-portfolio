Hi, I'm Isaac.

As a soon-to-be graduate from the University of California, Los Angeles with a Bachelor of Science in Physiological Science (Spring 2023), I bring a unique blend of medical knowledge and practical experience to the table. I worked as a Medical Intern at Space Exploration Technologies (SpaceX) from April 2021 through May 2023, where I was instrumental in developing medical equipment and training programs for commercial astronauts. My expertise lies in operations management, medical equipment engineering, and product development, which I have honed through hands-on experience and a focus on human factors, intelligent equipment engineering, and modern digital media design.

With a strong background in medicine, clinical experrience, and engineering, I am eager to leverage my skills and knowledge in a fast-paced environment where I can make a difference using cutting-edge medical technology. My passion lies in helping others, and I'm excited to continue to grow and develop my skills to better serve those around me.

Work

At Space Exploration Technologies (SpaceX), I have honed my skills in persistence and commitment to excellence in every role I’ve taken on. Starting as a member of the COVID-19 testing team, I demonstrated reliability and sought opportunities for growth, eventually volunteering for a challenging research qualification campaign leading up to the groundbreaking Inspiration 4 mission. Following the successful campaign, I assumed responsibility for research qualification and spearheaded the development and qualification of the SpaceX Medical Kit.

Through these experiences, I gained a strong understanding of the aerospace industry and the significance of ensuring safety and readiness in every aspect of space missions. These projects further solidified my passion for making a meaningful impact in the space industry and other forward-thinking industries.

In my time at SpaceX, my major projects included:

- Medical Hardware Qualification and Training

  • Managed INSCOP (on-orbit anti-emetic) research project for the all-civilian Inspiration 4 mission (Summer/Fall 2021)
  • Medical Kit flight build and training for Axiom-1 mission, working with NASA counterparts to qualify to ISS toxicity standard (Winter 2022)
  • Developed new medical kit bag and internal organizational design for improved stowage, access, and repacking (Spring 2022)
  • Redesigned med kit per crew feedback to optimize usability and reduce volume by 50% (Summer/Fall 2022)
  • Deleted major requirement for flight pharmaceutical qualification testing, removing ~35 parts from scope (Fall 2022)
  • Leading overhaul of med equipment procurement and qualification for simplified and accelerated flight readiness, navigating DEA compliance (Fall 2022-Present)

- Research Hardware - Qualification Ownership

  • Selected for and led a research equipment qual sprint for the all-civilian Inspiration 4 mission, qualifying 30 pieces of hardware in 13 days and enabling 8 successful research projects (Fall 2021)
  • Developing receipt pathway and research qual campaign for Polaris Dawn while assisting with research development; responsible for ~75 research parts in 12 research projects (Spring 2022-Present)

- Other

  • Poster Presenter at the 2022 HRP-IWS conference where I discussed our 2021 INSCOP study focus, efficacy data, and lessons learned. (Spring 2022)
  • Facilitated early development of company-wide COVID testing program automation, making testing accessible for 10,000 SpaceX employees across our 4 primary facilities. (Fall 2021)

Education

I am currently pursuing a degree in Physiological Science at UCLA, with an expected graduation in June 2023. My academic achievements include being recognized as a Gold Shield Scholar and receiving the Alumni Scholarship from UCLA's Alumni Association.

Outside of academic pursuits, I am actively engaged in a variety of extracurricular activities, including as a varsity skipper on the UCLA Club Sailing team, an avid fan of UCLA athletic programs as a member of the Den Ops club, and as a part-time photographer focusing on athletic events. I have also served as a dedicated member of the Student-Alumni Association, contributing to the planning, promotion, and successful execution of various student-initiated events. My notable accomplishments include the marketing and event coordination of Spring Sing 2022 — a campus-wide tatlent showcase for over 3000 students — as well as bi-monthly Alumni Networking events to prepare students for the workforce.

To complement my studies, I have obtained an IRB certificate in Biomedical Research and certifications in Basic Life Saving and CPR from the Red Cross. These experiences have played a significant role in my growth as a medical and research professional and have inspired me to pursue these areas even further. I’m eager to continue developing my skills and knowledge in these fields throughout my years in the industry.
